## Problem

The world produces over 380 billion plastic bottles annually, with less than 30% being recycled. I wanted to design a reusable water bottle that would encourage long-term use through thoughtful design and sustainable materials.

Key challenges:
- Making sustainability appealing, not preachy
- Creating a bottle people actually want to carry daily
- Balancing durability with lightweight design

## Process

### Research & Discovery
I started by interviewing 25 people about their water bottle habits. The insights were eye-opening:
- 78% owned reusable bottles but used them inconsistently
- Main barriers: forgetting to bring them, difficult to clean, not enjoyable to use

### Design Development
Based on the research, I focused on three key areas:
1. **Memory prompts** - Visual and tactile reminders
2. **Easy maintenance** - Wide mouth, dishwasher safe materials
3. **Desirability** - Beautiful enough that people want to show it off

### Prototyping
I created over 12 prototypes, testing everything from grip comfort to cleaning ease. The breakthrough came when I realized the bottle itself could be a conversation starter about sustainability.

## Outcome

The final design features:
- **Recycled aluminum body** with powder coating
- **Bamboo cap** with integrated carrying loop
- **Modular design** allowing customization
- **QR code** linking to sustainability impact tracker

**Results:**
- 94% of test users reported using it daily after 2 weeks
- 67% said it changed their perspective on single-use plastics
- Featured in Fast Company's "Most Innovative Design" list

## Key Learnings

- **Sustainability sells when it's beautiful first** - People need to want the object before they care about its environmental impact
- **Small details matter enormously** - The weight distribution and grip texture were just as important as the eco-friendly materials
- **Systems thinking** - The bottle was just one part of a larger behavior change system
